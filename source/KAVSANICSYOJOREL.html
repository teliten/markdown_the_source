Aulis**_[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iphigenia_in_Aulis#cite_note-1) ([Ancient Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greek_language "Ancient Greek language"): Ἰφιγένεια ἐν Αὐλίδι, _Iphigeneia en Aulidi_; variously translated, including the Latin _Iphigenia in Aulide_) is the last of the extant works by the playwright [Euripides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euripides "Euripides"). Written between 408, after _[Orestes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orestes_(play) "Orestes (play)")_, and 406 BC, the year of Euripides' death, the play was first produced the following year[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iphigenia_in_Aulis#cite_note-2) in a trilogy with _[The Bacchae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Bacchae "The Bacchae")_ and _[Alcmaeon in Corinth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alcmaeon_in_Corinth "Alcmaeon in Corinth")_ by his son or nephew, Euripides the Younger,[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iphigenia_in_Aulis#cite_note-3) and won first place at the [City Dionysia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dionysia "Dionysia") in Athens. - The play revolves around [Agamemnon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agamemnon "Agamemnon"), the leader of the Greek coalition before and during the [Trojan War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trojan_War "Trojan War"), and his decision to sacrifice his daughter, [Iphigenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iphigenia "Iphigenia"), to appease the goddess [Artemis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artemis "Artemis") and allow his troops to set sail to preserve their honour in battle against [Troy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Troy "Troy").
